The Spooky History of Halloween

Halloween, or All Hallows' Eve, is an annual holiday celebrated on October 31. Its origins can be traced back to the ancient Celtic festival of Samhain. At that time, it was believed that the boundary between the living and the dead blurred, allowing spirits to walk the earth.

Traditions and Customs

Over the centuries, Halloween evolved into a secular holiday with a variety of traditions. Trick-or-treating became popular in North America in the 1920s and 30s. Dressing in costumes is also a major component, with people of all ages enjoying the chance to portray characters, monsters, and more.

Modern Celebrations

Today, Halloween is a multi-billion dollar industry that includes everything from haunted houses and pumpkin carving contests to elaborate decorations and themed parties. It's a night for creativity and community, bringing people together for a bit of harmless fun and fright.

Halloween Activities

Popular ways to celebrate include:

  1. Going trick-or-treating
  2. Carving jack-o'-lanterns
  3. Watching horror movies
  4. Visiting a haunted house
